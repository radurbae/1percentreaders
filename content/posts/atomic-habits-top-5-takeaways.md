## Book Notes: Atomic Habits by James Clear

After finishing _Atomic Habits_, here are the ideas that stuck with me the most.

### 1. The 1% Rule

> "If you get 1% better each day for one year, you'll end up 37 times better by the time you're done."

This is the core philosophy of our community — small improvements compound.

### 2. Identity-Based Habits

Don't focus on _what_ you want to achieve. Focus on _who_ you want to become.

- ❌ "I want to read more books"
- ✅ "I am a reader"

### 3. The Four Laws of Behavior Change

| Law                | Strategy                  |
| ------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Make it obvious    | Put a book on your pillow |
| Make it attractive | Join a reading community  |
| Make it easy       | Start with 5 pages        |
| Make it satisfying | Track your streak         |

### 4. Environment Design

Your environment shapes your behavior more than motivation does. Make the good habits easy and the bad habits hard.

### 5. The Plateau of Latent Potential

Progress isn't always visible. You might be reading for months without feeling smarter — but the knowledge is compounding beneath the surface.
