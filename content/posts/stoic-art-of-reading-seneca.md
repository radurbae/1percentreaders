## What Seneca Taught Me About Reading

Seneca, the great Stoic philosopher, had surprisingly modern advice about reading.

### Quality Over Quantity

> "It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a good deal of it." — Seneca

Seneca would argue that reading 50 books poorly is worse than reading 5 books deeply.

### The Practice of Digesting

Seneca recommended reading the same passages repeatedly until they become part of your thinking:

- **Read** a passage once for understanding
- **Re-read** it for depth
- **Reflect** on how it applies to your life
- **Write** about it in your own words

### Reading as Medicine

The Stoics viewed reading as medicine for the soul. Each book is a prescription:

- Feeling anxious? Read Marcus Aurelius
- Lacking courage? Read Epictetus
- Seeking wisdom? Read Seneca himself

### My Practice

Every morning, I read one letter from Seneca's _Letters from a Stoic_. Just one. Then I sit with it for 10 minutes before starting my day.

It's not about speed. It's about _absorption_.
